City Latitude Vs. Wind Speed

Again we see greater data clusters around the northern hemisphere, likely due to more population centers. As a point of comparison, Berlin, arguably the beginning of northern Europe, is at 52 degree north. Tierra del Fuego, the literal end of the Americas, is at 55 degree south. We also see that wind speed and latitude do not correlate. Wind speed, like humidity, is a factor of geography and ocean currents. Most data points for measurable wind speeds are low, likely because areas with high average wind speeds can only support population density with great effort.
